Disney Parks

Mobile Order
State Enhancements

Client Disneyland & Walt Disney World
Role Product Designer (Solo)
Platforms iOS · Android
Year 2019 – 2020

Usage was up. But food was being wasted. Guests weren't picking up their orders on time — and the pickup flow wasn't communicating clearly enough to help them.

Since the launch of Mobile Order in 2018, my team had been tracking usage data across the Parks. In 2019, a clear pattern emerged: users were not picking up their orders in a timely manner after they were ready, resulting in operational issues for in-park restaurants — wasted food and a rise in Guest complaints.

This project became an opportunity to take a second look at the Pickup flow and streamline the entire experience through thoughtful UX and visual updates.

My Role

Full ownership —
design & operations.

For this project, I was responsible for all aspects of design: concepting, iterating, prototyping, and documentation. I also created presentations and pitched designs to stakeholders and leadership. I worked with one Design Lead who oversaw the project and one Visual Designer from our team.

Beyond design, I worked closely with product management, business partners, and engineering to keep the project on track. This included project planning, budgeting projections, resource allocation, and weekly syncs with both product and engineering to collect feedback and keep all teams aligned.

The Problem

"Where's my food?"

The biggest pain point — from both Guests and stakeholders — was the pickup experience. Directions weren't landing, and the progress bar wasn't communicating state changes clearly enough.

Users

Confused by the flow

Guests reported they often didn't understand how the Pickup flow worked — they weren't aware of pickup instructions, and the progress bar states weren't clear. Even users who understood the bar said state changes were too subtle.

Stakeholders

Operational waste

Park restaurants — the Mobile Order stakeholders — reported a surge in unclaimed meals. Orders that weren't picked up in time had to be discarded per health code guidelines, resulting in food waste and resource loss.

Design Direction

Make waiting delightful.

According to restaurant data, the average Mobile Order completes within a few minutes of placement. This created a unique design scenario: we needed Guests to keep their screens awake and pay attention to order status — but without feeling like a chore.

The insight: make it fun to watch. I wanted to entice Guests to keep the app open by incorporating something delightful and eye-catching that also communicated status clearly.

Storyboarding

The existing Pickup flow used static states to instruct users and show order status. I saw this as an opportunity to introduce a narrative animation — a "story" that would weave both instructions and status updates into a sequence easily interpreted at a glance.

My initial storyboards were abstract, but the idea sold itself when I brought it to leadership. I then enlisted a Visual Designer to help flesh out the storyline and create the final animations.

— Project retrospective notes
Outcome

Launched Q1 2020 —
then COVID hit.

Mobile Order State Enhancements launched in Q1 of 2020. Based on initial feedback, Guests found the new pickup experience significantly more straightforward and enjoyable.

A note on timing

Unfortunately, due to COVID-19 the Parks were closed in early 2020 — just weeks after launch. This prevented us from collecting meaningful longitudinal data to measure the full impact of the enhancements on pickup rates, food waste, and Guest satisfaction. The work was solid; the timing was not.
