Disney Parks

Mobile Order
Launch

Client Disneyland & Walt Disney World
Role Interaction Designer
Platforms iOS · Android
Year 2017

Two of the most visited theme parks on earth were facing a growing challenge: as attendance climbed into the tens of millions, so did the lines — for rides, shops, and food.

28.6M Disneyland annual visitors (2018)
58.4M Walt Disney World annual visitors (2018)
2017 Year Mobile Order launched

Disneyland Resort and Walt Disney World are two of the most visited theme parks in the entire world. As attendance in the Parks increases, so do the lines for rides, shops, and food. My team saw an opportunity to improve our Guests' in-park dining experiences by introducing the Mobile Order platform — reducing lines and shortening wait times at quick-service restaurants (QSRs) throughout the Parks.

Design challenge

How might we improve the in-park dining experience?

My Role

Interaction Designer,
end-to-end.

I was brought on as an Interaction Designer, primarily focused on finalizing UX deliverables such as end-to-end user flows, comps, and documentation. I also provided engineering support and QA testing before the product went live.

Because this was my first major project at Disney, I used the opportunity to deeply understand Disney's design principles, philosophies, and strategies — and how they translated into a consumer mobile product used by millions.

Launch deliverables

Ship fast, iterate thoughtfully.

On launch, Mobile Order served to meet the core requirement: allowing Guests to quickly order food in-park and skip the line. My team focused priorities on getting the feature shipped quickly, with additional enhancements queued in the backlog for future planning cycles.

Outcome

Consistent growth,
positive reception.

Since launch in 2017, Mobile Order has seen consistent user growth month-over-month at both Disneyland and Walt Disney World. The feature increased food-order sales across the Parks as Guests embraced the convenience of ordering while in line for rides or commuting between attractions.

Guest feedback

General feedback from Guests has been overwhelmingly positive. Most reported that Mobile Order saves significant time by letting them place food orders while already in line or moving through the Park — keeping the magic going rather than spending it waiting at a counter.

As the feature continues to improve over time, the team's goal remains the same: help Guests spend less time worrying about food, and more time enjoying everything the Parks have to offer.
