Disney Parks

Mobile Order
Menu Redesign

Client Disneyland & Walt Disney World
Role Lead UX Designer
Platforms iOS · Android
Year 2018 – 2019

After a successful launch, the data spoke clearly: the Menu was the biggest pain point — users couldn't navigate it, and there were no images of the food.

After the launch of Mobile Order, my team began tracking user data and feedback. Guests pointed out that the Menu was the biggest friction point — it lacked navigation and food imagery. My team and I took that signal and kicked off the next phase: a full Menu Redesign.

My Role

Owning design end-to-end.

I was in charge of all major UX deliverables: user flows, concepts, final comps, and documentation. I created multiple prototypes throughout the project — including initial concepting prototypes, user testing prototypes, and motion studies for documentation. I also helped establish the visual direction for the icon set used for each Menu category.

This project included travel to Walt Disney World for on-site user testing, which gave me the opportunity to strengthen relationships with our product and engineering teams based in Orlando. I collaborated with one Lead Designer and one Senior Designer throughout the engagement.

Discovery & Workshopping

Start with the right question.

Before wireframes or concepts, I made sure I completely understood all business requirements. Based on what I heard from stakeholders and our Product/Engineering teams, I kicked off the project with a single guiding question:

Design challenge

How might we make mobile ordering easier and more enjoyable for our guests?

This question led my team into a workshop session where we audited the existing Menu and began brainstorming solutions for a more enjoyable experience.

Finding the focus

At the conclusion of our workshop, we aligned on three focus areas:

  1. 01 Navigation — Users should be able to easily browse between all menu items and variations across the Parks' available restaurants.
  2. 02 Visuals — The MVP launch had no food photography on the Menu. The only way to see an item was to tap into its Detail page — a frustrating experience when browsing on the move.
  3. 03 Brand Identity — The Mobile Order experience should feel fun and whimsical — distinctly Disney. We wanted to bring that magic into the product.
Concepting

Three directions, one winner.

I created three distinct concepts to start discussions with design leadership and product. The goal was to identify which interaction pattern would best serve our Guests.

Concept 01

Dropdown

Each menu category becomes a collapsible dropdown — allowing users to jump between categories without excessive scrolling. Clean, but similar to familiar patterns.

Concept 02

Carousel

Category navigation anchors to the top of the page as the user scrolls — always present and accessible. A more dynamic, thumb-friendly experience.

Concept 03

Hub & Spoke

A "hub" page lists all categories; tapping one leads to a dedicated page of items. Cleaner structure but adds an extra navigation step.

User Testing

We flew to
Walt Disney World.

Taking feedback from leadership and product, I narrowed the concepts to two (Dropdown was removed due to its similarity with Carousel) and built interactive prototypes loaded onto iOS devices for testing.

With prototypes in hand, my team and I flew to Walt Disney World to conduct in-park user testing — meeting Guests in their natural environment.

14 Users tested
14/14 Preferred the Carousel concept
Feb 2019 Official launch date

The results surprised everyone — no one expected that every single tester would prefer the same interaction pattern. One captured quote perfectly encapsulated what I wanted to accomplish.

— Research debrief notes, Walt Disney World

All 14 users chose the Carousel. Their reasoning was consistent: it encouraged immediate interaction with food categories and made it easy to quickly browse through the Menu — especially while on the move in a busy park.

Final Deliverables

Polished, shipped, celebrated.

With a clear signal from testing, I proceeded with polishing the Carousel concept and preparing design files for handoff to our development team.

Launch & Press

The Menu Redesign officially launched at both parks in February 2019 and was covered by both the Disney Parks Blog and WDWNT. The Carousel navigation and food photography transformed how Guests discovered and ordered food in-park.
