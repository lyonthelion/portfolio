Disney Parks

Dine Reservation
Add-Ons

Client Disneyland & Walt Disney World
Role Product Designer
Platform Responsive Web
Year 2019

Guests celebrate birthdays, anniversaries, and milestones at Disney every day. But to add a celebration to their reservation, they had to call us.

Disneyland and Disney World combined have over 40 full-service dining locations serving the tens of millions of guests that visit the parks every year. Reservations are required at nearly all of them — walk-ins are generally not accepted given the volume. Guests plan ahead, often around special occasions.

Before this project, guests could make dine reservations online — but could only select dates, times, and party size. Any special celebrations had to be added by calling Disney directly, putting pressure on call center staff and creating unnecessary friction for guests who just wanted to mark a milestone.

Opportunity

We saw an opportunity to enhance the reservation experience by allowing guests to add "celebrations" (add-ons) online — ahead of their visit.

The Problem

40+ restaurants.
Zero self-service celebrations.

For Guests

Unnecessary friction

Wanting to add a birthday note to a dinner reservation required a phone call — an extra step that felt out of place in an otherwise digital-first booking experience.

For Call Centers

High volume, low complexity

Call center agents were handling a significant volume of calls that could be easily resolved through self-service — taking capacity away from more complex Guest needs.

For the Business

Missed upsell opportunity

Celebration add-ons represent a natural upsell moment. Without a digital flow, that revenue potential was being left on the table — or lost entirely.

Design Constraints

Designing within an older system.

To meet the budget requirements set out for this project, we had to reuse as many existing online components as possible to reduce development costs. This meant designing within the constraint of an older UI platform — a challenge that revealed significant limitations in the existing design system.

Rather than seeing this as a blocker, my team and I used it as a creative constraint — forcing us to get more inventive with our solutioning and find elegant approaches within a rigid system.

Design

Adding celebrations
to the reservation flow.

The goal was to integrate the add-on experience naturally into the existing dine reservation flow — without disrupting the primary booking journey. The add-on step needed to feel like a natural extension, not a detour.

Outcome

Launched November 2019,
on budget.

Result

Disney Parks Dine Reservation Add-Ons launched in November 2019, giving guests the ability to add celebration experiences directly to their reservations online — reducing call center load, improving the guest experience, and opening a new upsell channel for the business.
